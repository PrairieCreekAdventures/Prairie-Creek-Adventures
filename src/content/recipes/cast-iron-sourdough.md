This is the loaf I make almost every Saturday. One rise, one shape, one bake. The Dutch oven does all the hard work.

## Ingredients

- 500g bread flour
- 350g warm water
- 100g active sourdough starter
- 10g fine sea salt

## Method

Mix everything in a large bowl with your hands until it comes together into a shaggy dough. Cover with a damp cloth.

Leave it somewhere warm for 8 to 12 hours, until nearly doubled. The exact time depends on your kitchen. In summer, it's fast. In winter, it takes its time.

Tip the dough onto a lightly floured counter. Fold the edges up to the middle, flip it over, and let it rest seam-side down for 20 minutes.

Preheat your Dutch oven at 475°F for 30 minutes.

Score the top of the loaf with a sharp knife. Lower it into the hot pot (use parchment paper for an easy transfer), cover, and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the lid and bake another 20 to 25 minutes, until the crust is deep mahogany.

Cool on a rack for at least an hour before slicing.
