A deeply comforting soup that asks for almost nothing in the way of technique — just time, good butter, and a willingness to let the browning happen properly.

## Ingredients

- 1 large butternut squash, about 3 lbs
- 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 small yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves of garlic
- 10 sage leaves, plus a handful for garnish
- 4 cups good stock (chicken or vegetable)
- Crème fraîche, for finishing
- Pepitas
- Sea salt and freshly ground pepper

## Method

Heat the oven to 400°F. Halve the squash, scoop the seeds, rub the flesh with olive oil. Roast cut-side down for 45 minutes, until tender.

While it roasts, melt the butter in a heavy pot over medium-low heat. Let it foam and quiet. When it turns golden and nutty, add the onion. Cook slowly, stirring now and then, for 10 to 15 minutes.

Scoop the squash into the pot. Add the garlic, the sage, and the stock. Simmer for 20 minutes. Purée with a hand blender. Taste carefully — it will want more salt than you'd guess.

Finish each bowl with a spoonful of crème fraîche, crisped sage, and pepitas.

## Notes

The brown butter is the whole point. Do not rush it.
