A soft, floral preserve that captures the last of the pears. Lovely on toast, essential on a winter cheese board.

## Ingredients

- 4 lbs ripe pears, peeled and diced
- 1 cup honey
- ¼ cup lemon juice
- 1 vanilla bean, split
- Pinch of salt

## Method

Combine everything in a heavy pot. Let it sit for an hour — the fruit will release its juice.

Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring often. Cook for about an hour, until thickened and glossy. The preserve is ready when a spoonful on a cold plate holds its shape.

Ladle into sterilized jars, leaving ½ inch of headspace. Seal and process in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes for shelf-stable storage, or simply refrigerate and use within three weeks.

## Notes

Pears vary in sweetness. Taste as you go and adjust the honey.
