A cookie that keeps for weeks in a tin and gets better after a day or two. The rosemary is subtle — you're meant to wonder what that flavor is.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
- ½ cup sugar, plus more for sprinkling
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh rosemary
- ½ teaspoon salt

## Method

Cream the butter and sugar until pale. Stir in the rosemary and salt. Add the flour and mix until just combined. Do not overwork it.

Pat the dough into a disc, wrap, and chill for 30 minutes.

Roll to ½ inch thick. Cut into rectangles, prick each with a fork, sprinkle with sugar.

Bake at 325°F for 18 to 22 minutes, until the edges are just starting to turn golden. Cool on the pan.

Store in an airtight tin. They are best on day two.
