The galette is the pie for people who do not feel like making a pie. It is rustic on purpose. The edges fold up however they fold up. Nothing needs to be perfect.

## Ingredients

For the crust:
- 1¼ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ½ cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
- 3 to 4 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:
- 3 medium apples, thinly sliced
- ¼ cup brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
- 1 tablespoon flour
- 1 egg, beaten (for the wash)
- Turbinado sugar

## Method

Make the crust first. Whisk the dry ingredients. Cut in the butter until the mixture looks like coarse sand with some larger pieces of butter still visible. Add ice water a tablespoon at a time until it comes together. Pat into a disc, wrap, and chill for at least an hour.

Toss the apples with brown sugar, lemon, cardamom, and flour.

Roll the dough into a rough circle, about 12 inches across. Pile the apples in the center, leaving a 2-inch border. Fold the edges up and over, pleating as you go. Brush with egg wash and sprinkle generously with turbinado.

Bake at 400°F for 40 to 45 minutes, until the crust is deeply golden and the apples are tender.

## Notes

Serve warm, with vanilla ice cream or a cold pour of heavy cream.
