There are two kinds of sourdough. There is the kind that demands a scale, a thermometer, a stretch-and-fold schedule, and a hydration calculator. And there is the kind I make.

I am not going to tell you that the first kind is wrong. It makes extraordinary bread. But for most of us — tired, busy, hungry — it is more commitment than the bread is worth.

This is the other kind. One rise. One shape. A cast iron pot. Bread by dinner.

## The starter

If you do not have one, ask a neighbor. Every neighborhood has one person keeping a jar going. It will be thrilled to be shared. If you truly cannot find one, the instant yeast version of this is also excellent and takes four hours instead of twelve.

## The loaf

Mix 500g of bread flour, 350g of warm water, 100g of active starter, and 10g of salt in a large bowl. Stir with your hands until it comes together. It will be shaggy and unimpressive. Good.

Cover. Leave it alone for eight to twelve hours, depending on how warm your kitchen is. It should roughly double.

Shape it — just pull the edges up to the middle, flip it over, and let it rest on the counter for twenty minutes. Score the top with a sharp knife. Into a preheated Dutch oven at 475°F, lid on for twenty minutes, lid off for another twenty.

Let it cool before you cut it. This is the hardest step.
