I have made it a hundred times. My grandmother made it a thousand. It asks almost nothing of you: a butternut split and roasted, a half-stick of good butter taken past golden, a handful of sage from whatever corner of the garden is still giving. And then, most importantly, time.

## Why this soup

The magic is not in the ingredients. It is in what happens when you brown the butter properly — when you stand over it with a wooden spoon and wait for the moment it stops foaming and goes quiet and nutty. That is where the depth lives.

## What you'll need

- 1 large butternut squash, about 3 lbs
- 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 small yellow onion
- 3 cloves of garlic
- 10 sage leaves, plus a few extra for garnish
- 4 cups good chicken or vegetable stock
- Salt, pepper, a little cream if you like
- Pepitas, for scattering

## Method

Heat the oven to 400°F. Halve the squash, scoop the seeds, rub the flesh with olive oil, and roast cut-side down for about 45 minutes, until the skin gives when you press it.

While it roasts, melt the butter in a heavy pot over medium-low heat. Let it foam, then quiet. When it turns golden and smells like toasted hazelnuts, add the onion and cook slowly, stirring occasionally, for ten or fifteen minutes.

Scoop the squash flesh out of its skin and into the pot. Add the garlic, the sage, and the stock. Simmer for twenty minutes. Purée with a hand blender until smooth. Taste for salt — it will need more than you think.

Finish with a swirl of cream, a few crisped sage leaves, and a handful of pepitas.

---

Serve it with dark bread and good butter, to someone you love.
